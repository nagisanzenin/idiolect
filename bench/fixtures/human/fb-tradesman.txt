Got a call at 6:40 this morning, water heater let go in a duplex over on Maple. Forty gallons in the downstairs unit's ceiling by the time I got there.

Here's the thing nobody wants to hear. That tank was 14 years old. The anode rod had maybe 3 years of sacrifice left in it when the place changed owners, and nobody's touched it since.

$18 part. Twenty minutes with a breaker bar, once a decade. Thats the whole job.

The ceiling repair estimate came in at $2,300 and their insurance is fighting the claim because the tank was "past service life."

Check your water heater's install date. It's on the sticker, top left usually. If it starts with 201 and you cant remember replacing an anode rod, call somebody. Doesn't have to be me.
