We killed our internship program's GPA cutoff in January. Some numbers from the first cohort since people asked.

Applications went from 210 to 340. The interview-pass rate was flat, 22% versus 21% the prior year, which was the thing legal was nervous about. It did not drop.

Two of the five interns we hired would have been filtered out under the old 3.5 rule. One of them found a race condition in our billing retry logic during week 6. She had a 2.9 and a part-time job through most of school, which in hindsight explains both numbers.

The cutoff was never measuring ability. It was measuring who could afford to treat college as their only job.

Happy to share the rubric we replaced it with, it's one page and stolen shamelessly from a peer company's public one.
