Finally sorted the flickering on my HO layout's yard lights, posting because three threads here got me 80% of the way and none had the ending.

Symptoms: six LED lamp posts on a shared bus, random flicker on two of them, worse when the switcher crossed frog 7. Everyone said dirty track. Cleaned it, no change.

It was the bus splice under the yard throat. A 2014-era suitcase connector I'd have sworn was fine. Resistance read 0.4 ohms cold and jumped past 3 when I flexed the board, which matches the seasonal humidity swing here in Sacramento.

Soldered a proper pigtail, heat shrink, done. Four evenings of chasing a 90-cent part.

If your flicker correlates with a specific loco crossing a specific frog, check your splices before your railheads. The frog was just the vibration source.
