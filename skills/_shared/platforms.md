# Platform grammar (2026)

*What native looks like, per venue. A voice adapts to the room without changing who it is (audience design — Bell 1984). Sourced from platform-norms research 2026-07; re-verify yearly, these decay.*

| Platform | Native length | Emoji | Hashtags | Links | Caps culture |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| x | <280 chars, or long-form 500–1500 chars | 0–1 | 0 (dead for reach) | in first reply ("link below") | lowercase-casual is high-status |
| linkedin | 900–1800 chars, hook in first 210 | 0–3 functional | 0–3 niche, at end | "link in comments" | sentence case; CAPS = guru |
| reddit | 150–600 words, real paragraphs | ~0 (emoji = ad tell) | never | per-sub rules; often comments-only | sentence case casual |
| hn | Show HN 100–300 words, plain text | zero tolerance | never | the post is the link | plain; adjectives get mocked |
| facebook | 50–250 words, conversational | moderate is normal | 0–1 | most groups gate links | normal |
| instagram | hook in first 125 chars; carousels 800–1500 | 1–5, mid-sentence | 2–3 max (capped Nov 2025); caption SEO > tags | "link in bio" only | lowercase-creator |

## Per-platform judgment calls

- **X**: single long-form post now beats a thread; "🧵👇" is a self-identifying growth-hacker badge. First line carries everything (only ~280 chars show pre-fold). Author-answered replies are the ranking currency — end where a reader has something specific to ask.
- **LinkedIn**: the algorithm itself demotes AI-slop and broetry formatting (2025–26). One-line-paragraph poetry with a fake-vulnerable arc is over. What works: a real number in the first line, specifics in the middle, an offer to compare notes at the end. Audiences report spotting AI "in three sentences" — and >half of long posts are AI-assisted, so the bar for texture is high.
- **Reddit**: 90/10 participation rule; disclose you're the founder; frame promo as lessons/data with the product incidental ("$410 MRR, 9 users, churn came from onboarding" beats any feature list). r/programming bans AI-generated content outright; mods eyeball em-dash density and posting history. Never repost identical text across subs — that's what `overlap` is for.
- **HN**: honesty about limitations IS the marketing ("Known limitations: personas drift after ~50 posts"). "Excited to announce" is near-guaranteed hostility. Engage criticism with data in comments; comments outrank votes.
- **Facebook groups**: value first, product only in approved threads or when asked. "Comment INFO and I'll DM you" is burned funnel-bait.
- **Instagram/TikTok**: captions are search surfaces now — keywords in prose, not tag walls. Lowercase reads native; Title Case reads ad.
- **Substack Notes**: relatability beats brilliance; restacking others is core etiquette; "subscribe for more" endings punished.
- **YouTube community**: attach an image or poll; polls are the native engagement unit, not "Thoughts?".

## Burned patterns (recognized and mocked, 2025–26 — never generate)

- "I asked ChatGPT to… and the answer shocked me"
- Broetry (one line per sentence + fake vulnerability arc: "I cried in my car. Then I closed the deal.")
- "Thoughts?" / "Agree?" / "Who else?" endings; polls-as-bait outside YT
- "🧵👇", "1/", "Like if you agree, RT if…"
- Rocket/sparkle emoji clusters; 5-bullet emoji listicles
- "Comment [WORD] and I'll DM you the guide"
- Hashtag walls (#fyp #viral; 20-tag IG blocks)
- "Excited to announce 🎉" / press-release register in a personal feed
- "I built X because I was frustrated with Y" as a bare template (fine as a fact, burned as a skeleton — needs numbers and a wrinkle)
- Fake-specific vulnerability ("My hands were shaking when I hit send…")
- **The humanizer house style** — uniformly short sentences, aggressive casualness, zero em dashes everywhere. Scrubbing tells without adding a person converges on a new tell. That's the whole reason voices exist.
