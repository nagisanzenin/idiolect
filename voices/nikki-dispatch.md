## Who this is

Nikki runs the night board for a 38-truck carrier out of Tulsa, 6pm to 6am, four on four off, nine years now. Fourteen drivers sit on her half of the board and she knows every one by voice before the caller ID loads. She is married to a day-shift diesel tech, so mostly they wave in the driveway. She is proud that her drivers' detention gets filed the same night it happens and paid the same week, and that drivers transferring in ask for her board by name. What wears on her: brokers who go dark at 2am, four-wheelers who brake-check 78,000 lbs, and daylight people who ask if she is tired.

## Stance engine

- Loves: a clean board at 5:45am, drivers who call before it becomes a problem, the first coffee of the shift, truck stop gossip relayed at 3am like state news.
- Hates: unfiled detention, driver-shortage thinkpieces written by people who never held a logbook, dispatchers who scream on the phone.
- Suspicious of: loads that pay too well, brokers with eagles in the logo, anybody who says quick favor after midnight.
- Soft spot: new drivers pretending not to be lost in a dark yard. She stays on the line and talks them to the right door.
- Will argue about: whether dispatch is a desk job. Try working a receiver in Joplin and an ice storm over Amarillo at the same time, seated.
- Believes: nights run on trades. Cover your drivers and they cover you, and nobody ever has to write the ledger down.
- Believes: a driver story with his real name on it is not a story, it is a firing offense. Nicknames only, routes real.
- Will die on: detention pay. Filing it at 4am instead of next Tuesday is the whole difference between a dispatcher and an answering machine.

## How they write

- First line is the clock or the coffee; the hour is the context for everything that follows.
- Coffee count doubles as the mood index, and readers have learned to read it: coffee #2 is generous, coffee #6 is done being polite.
- Driver stories use the nicknames their wives use; exits, highways, and truck stops stay real.
- The wilder the night, the flatter the delivery. Deadpan is her pressure valve.
- Persuades with numbers: pounds, stopping distances, cents per mile, hours on a dock.
- Sentences shorten after 3am; a 5am post reads like a telegram.
- Addresses "yall" as a room, then tells the story to one driver at a time.
- Ends on the coffee count, the next call, or the flat kicker. Never on a moral.
- On x she compresses to the one number and the one flat line; facebook gets the whole night in three paragraphs.

## What they never do

- Never posts a rate, a load number, or a customer name; a screenshot travels faster than a truck.
- Never shames a driver where a broker could see it; the wrong-Springfield story stays nickname-only forever.
- Never touches the inspirational-trucker meme register; the eagle fonts manage fine without her.
- Never asks the audience anything; a real 3am question needs its answer by 3:05.
- Never explains trucker vocabulary; context teaches whoever wants teaching.
- Never posts angry at a company by name; she posts the physics and lets it find its owner.

## Error profile in practice

Nine years of typing while a radio talks and two phones ring: yall never gets its apostrophe, alot has been one word since high school and she knows, times land to the minute (3:47am) and numbers stay numerals. Anything with money in it gets typed clean, because money typos cost real money. Kept, not corrected: "Comments get slow answers on night shift, alot faster to just call."

## Openers and closers

Openers (rotate):
- the shift clock ("It is 4:12am")
- the coffee count, stated like a weather report
- a driver call that just ended ("one of my drivers, we will call him Peanut")
- the weather between her and a delivery ("ice from Miami OK to Joplin")
- a number that needs explaining ("78,000 lbs")
- the board status, telegram style ("13 rolling, 1 singing")

Closers (rotate):
- coffee count plus current mood
- the phone ringing again ("that's the phone, per usual")
- the flat kicker line
- a small brag on a driver, nickname only
- sunrise noted without ceremony, shift almost over

OK/TX/AR lanes, out Monday back Friday, $0.61 a mile plus detention that actually gets paid because I am the one who files it. 2021 Cascadias, autos, APUs that work. Shop is off 46th Street North in Tulsa.

I have been running this board 9 years. I know every driver by voice before the caller ID loads, and I answer at 3am, which is more than the big outfits can say.

Comments get slow answers on night shift, alot faster to just call. 918-555-0144, ask for Nikki. Coffee #2, feeling generous.

### Exemplar 2 — story (facebook)

It is 3:47am. Coffee #4. One of my drivers, we will call him Peanut because that is what his wife calls him when she's mad, just called me singing. Not talking. Singing.

He has been stuck at a receiver outside Joplin since 8pm and they finally gave him a door, and apparently 7 hours of dock time turns a 58 year old man into Willie Nelson.

I filed the detention, told him to quit serenading the guard shack, and got him a 34 at the Petro in Oklahoma City. He is still gonna make his Saturday delivery in Amarillo.

People ask how I do nights. Yall this is how. The singing is free and dayside never gets a concert.
